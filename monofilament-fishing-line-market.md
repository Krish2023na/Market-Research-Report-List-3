<p><ul><li>Below 0.20 mm</li><li>0.20-0.40 mm</li><li>0.40-0.80 mm</li><li>Above 0.80 mm</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Monofilament fishing lines are categorized based on their diameter, which influences their strength and durability. Lines below 0.20 mm are typically used for light tackle fishing in situations where finesse is important. The 0.20-0.40 mm range is popular for general fishing applications, offering a balance between strength and flexibility. Lines in the 0.40-0.80 mm range are used for more heavy-duty fishing, such as deep-sea or freshwater trolling. Lines above 0.80 mm are the thickest and provide the highest strength for handling large, powerful fish.</p></p>
