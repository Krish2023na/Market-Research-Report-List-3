<p><p>Spunbond nonwovens can be categorized into different market types based on the material used in their production. The most common types include polypropylene, polyethylene, and polyester spunbond nonwovens. Polypropylene spunbond nonwovens are known for their durability and resistance to moisture, making them ideal for applications such as medical and hygiene products. Polyethylene spunbond nonwovens offer a softer texture and are often used in products like protective clothing and agricultural covers. Polyester spunbond nonwovens are valued for their strength and thermal stability, making them suitable for applications in geotextiles and automotive materials. Other materials such as viscose or cellulose may also be used to produce spunbond nonwovens for specific applications.</p></p>
