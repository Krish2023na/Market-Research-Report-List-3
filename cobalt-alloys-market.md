<p><p>Cobalt alloys are categorized into two main types: Cobalt-Base Wear-Resistant Alloys and Cobalt-Base High-Temperature Alloys. Cobalt-Base Wear-Resistant Alloys are used in applications where resistance to wear and corrosion is essential, such as in cutting tools and biomedical implants. On the other hand, Cobalt-Base High-Temperature Alloys are designed to maintain their mechanical properties at elevated temperatures, making them ideal for use in gas turbine engines and industrial furnaces. Both types of cobalt alloys play a vital role in various industries due to their unique properties.</p></p>
