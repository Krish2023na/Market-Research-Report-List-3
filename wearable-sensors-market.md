<p><p>Wearable sensors are classified into two categories: invasive and non-invasive sensors. Invasive sensors are implanted under the skin or within the body to directly measure physiological parameters. On the other hand, non-invasive sensors are worn externally on the body to monitor vital signs without penetrating the skin. Both types of sensors are widely used in the healthcare industry for various applications such as monitoring chronic conditions, tracking fitness levels, and promoting overall wellness.</p></p>
