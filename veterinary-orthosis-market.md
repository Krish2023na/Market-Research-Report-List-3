<p><p>Veterinary orthoses are specialized devices used to support, protect, or correct abnormalities in animals' limbs. There are two main types of veterinary orthoses based on the location of the limb they are designed for: forelimb and hindlimb orthoses. Forelimb orthoses are tailored for front legs, often used to treat conditions such as arthritis or soft tissue injuries. Hindlimb orthoses, on the other hand, are designed for back legs and are commonly used to address issues like hip dysplasia or ligament damage. Both types play a crucial role in improving the mobility and quality of life for animals.</p></p>
