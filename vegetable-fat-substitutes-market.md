<p><p>Vegetable fat substitutes come in three main types: Carbohydrate-based, Protein-based, and Fat-based substitutes. Carbohydrate-based substitutes are typically made from starches or fibers and provide a similar texture to fats in foods. Protein-based substitutes, on the other hand, use proteins such as soy or pea protein to mimic the creaminess of fats. Fat-based substitutes are usually made from vegetable oils and are used to replace traditional fats like butter or margarine in recipes. Each type of substitute offers a different texture and taste to cater to various dietary needs and preferences.</p></p>
