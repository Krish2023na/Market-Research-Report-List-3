<p><p>Vacuum aligners are designed to align and bond wafers in a vacuum environment, ensuring high precision and consistency. Atmosphere aligners, on the other hand, align wafers under atmospheric conditions, offering a different set of advantages like improved productivity and lower production costs. Both types of aligners play a crucial role in semiconductor manufacturing, with vacuum aligners being ideal for applications where ultra-precision is required, while atmosphere aligners are preferred for high-volume production processes.</p></p>
