<p><p>Microbial Fuel Cells (MFC) are devices that use bacteria to generate electricity from organic matter. There are two main types of MFCs in the market - Mediator Microbial Fuel Cells and Mediator-free Microbial Fuel Cells. In Mediator MFCs, a mediator compound is used to transfer electrons between the bacteria and the anode, while in Mediator-free MFCs, the bacteria directly transfer electrons to the anode. Both types have their own advantages and disadvantages in terms of efficiency, cost, and environmental impact.</p></p>
