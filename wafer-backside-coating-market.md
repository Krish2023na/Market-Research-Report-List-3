<p><p>Wafer backside coatings can be categorized into two main types: conductive and non-conductive. Conductive coatings are used to enhance electrical conductivity on the backside of the wafer, allowing for better grounding and heat dissipation during semiconductor manufacturing processes. Non-conductive coatings, on the other hand, are applied to prevent electrical conduction and short circuits on the backside of the wafer. Both types of coatings play a crucial role in ensuring the overall performance and reliability of semiconductor devices.</p></p>
