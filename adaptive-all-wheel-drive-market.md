<p><p>Adaptive All Wheel Drive systems come in two main market types: Automatic and Manual. Automatic systems use sensors to continuously monitor road conditions and distribute power to the wheels accordingly, providing seamless control and stability. On the other hand, Manual systems require the driver to manually engage the system based on their assessment of the road conditions. Both types offer enhanced traction and handling in varying driving conditions, but Automatic systems offer a more hands-off approach while Manual systems offer more control to the driver.</p></p>
