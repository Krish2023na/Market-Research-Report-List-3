<p><p>Mechanical LiDAR scanners use rotating mirrors to direct laser beams, allowing for high-resolution 3D mapping. These scanners are more common in automotive applications, providing accurate distance measurements and object detection. Solid State LiDAR, on the other hand, utilizes solid-state technology to emit laser pulses without moving parts, making them more durable and compact. They are gaining traction in the market due to their reliability and lower cost of production. Both types of LiDAR scanners play a crucial role in autonomous driving and remote sensing applications.</p></p>
